The electric locomotive has two head and two intermediate booster sections and is used to haul super-heavy long freight trains on flat terrain or heavy trains in mountainous terrain on sections with a complex profile. The use of two booster sections instead of two head sections improves the working conditions of the locomotive crew due to the ability to pass through all sections during movement and reduces the overall cost of the locomotives due to the absence of unnecessary cabins.

AA20

Russian Railways

3,652 hp


The SZD Class AA20 was a one-off experimental 4-14-4 steam locomotive constructed by the Soviet Union by Krupp and the Voroshilovgrad Locomotive Factory in 1934 for the Sovetskie Zheleznye Dorogi. The AA steam locomotive was the first and only locomotive in the world history with seven driving axles in one rigid frame. The steam locomotive's boiler was one of the largest locomotive boilers in Europe. Due to the sheer size of the locomotive, a number of measures were taken to improve the curve fit of the locomotive.
